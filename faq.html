Frequently Asked Questions

•   Who can participate?

We need tech developers, makers, game programmers, rebels that code who are up to the age of 26 years.

•   What is an TechNoFest?

TechNoFest is a short, intensive, brainstorming online event which focuses on the uniqueness of the idea submitted by young talents to innovate and create their own unique projects in order to generate fresh solutions to existing challenges.

•   When and Where is the event being held?

The event is completely online which will be conducted on goggle meets and the event is on 19th September.

•   Does the event cost anything?

No. You do not have to pay anything to register yourself for the TechNoFest.

•   Do I need to have any specific qualifications to be a participant for the TechNoFest?

If you have a revolutionary idea, you are welcome for the TechNoFest.

•   Can I change the theme my team is responding to?

You cannot change the selected theme once shortlisted for TechNoFest.

•   Judging criteria?

The judges will be looking at how unique and interesting your idea is, how close it is to being a product and how convincing your pitch presentation is.

•   Do I need to be in a team to participate?

No, we welcome both individual participants and teams of up to 5 members. In case you registered as an individual and would like to join a team, we will help facilitate that process.

•   Do I need to prepare anything before the event?

No preparation is required. However we do ask all registered participants to join our Slack channel and Whatsapp group.

•   When will I be notified if I receive an award?

After the event is over, the mentor team will carefully evaluate every submission received and decide how to best support them going forward. We will send an email to each team/individual on the same day with next steps.

•   How do I know the registration is confirmed and when to start the development phase?
The organizing team will approve and confirm your participation. Shortlisted teams will receive a confirmation email.

•   Do we need to have entire idea fully working?

The entire idea need not be fully implemented as it completely depends on how you present your idea which will be reviewed by the judges.
